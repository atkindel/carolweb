Center for Advancing Research through Online Learning

Since 2012, Stanford University has invested substantially in The Center for Advancing Research through Online Learning (CAROL) supports and seeds research in digital learning environments across the social sciences. The growing ubiquity of instruction conveyed in digital environments represents a unique opportunity to advance the science of human learning.

To support digital learning research, CAROL maintains an archive of research datasets derived from Stanford's online instructional offerings. In collaboration with the Office of the Vice Provost for Teaching and Learning (VPTL), CAROL manages data streams from course offerings on three digital platforms: Coursera, NovoEd, and Lagunita, Stanford's instance of the open-source EdX platform.

In addition to supporting digital learning research, CAROL is a forum for critical discourse about the ethics and politics of digital learning research

CAROL is affiliated with the Institute for Research in the Social Sciences and supports research activity across the School of Humanities and Sciences, the Graduate School of Education, and the School of Engineering. As part of Stanford's intramural science governance system, CAROL collaborates with faculty, the Office of General Counsel, and the Research Compliance Office to ensure that scientific inquiry in digital learning environments is performed with careful consideration of the rights and privacy of learners.
